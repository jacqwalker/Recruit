CV writing tips

  • Use action verbs such as developed, designed, created, implemented
  • Your CV should not read as a job description but rather include key achievements and where possible, add metrics e.g. reduced the monthly reporting close by 2 days generating £x in savings
  • Most hiring managers will review your CV in 30 seconds so make sure the first half page captures their attention
  • Your CV should not be longer than 2 and 1/2 pages
  • List qualifications, positions held, appointments, system experience, interests at the end of the CV, not the beginning
  • Have an opening statement - maximum 3 lines summarising your skills and experience to date e.g. Experienced finance manager with seven years PQE across multiple industries including retail, tech and FMCG. A strong business partner with extensive supply chain, FP&A and reporting experience.
